History of Jeffery's bay

1849: Founding

While the stories of the founding of Jeffrey's Bay range from lost captains building home's from their shipwrecks, to traders opening shop, to whalers settling down, it is generally accepted that Jeffrey's bay traces it origins to the year 1849. The same year that nearby Humansdorp was founded. Jeffrey's bay grew from a small settlement into a exceedingly popular holiday destination.

1960s: Surfing reputation

Jeffrey's Bay would garner a great deal of attention from the 1966 documentary "The Endless Summer" which showcased some of the greatest surfing spots in the world. By the 70s Jeffrey's Bay had a reputation as one of the premiere surf spots in the country, if not the world. Jeffrey's bay represented a unique challenge to seasoned surfers, who would try their luck at the Supertubes, as well as a relaxed spot for newer surfers to get more comfortable at places like the Point.

1980s: J-Bay open

In 1984 the first J-Bay open took place. An event part of the World Surf League and first won by Mark Occhilupo. The J-Bay Open has continued to this day with incredibly memorable events. Even at times grabbing world wide attention in 2015 when Mark Fanning fought a shark in a freak accident mid competition and walked away unharmed.
